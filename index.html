RC Car

Mechanical Designer

Objective

Design and construct a remote-controlled car that balances speed, stability, and power efficiency as part of a Machine Elements course project. The vehicle featured a PLA 3D-printed chassis, belt drive system, and servo-controlled Ackermann steering. Project responsibilities included timeline management, Bill of Materials tracking, and coordinating team progress.

Skills Used

  • CAD modeling in Onshape and FEA stress analysis in SolidWorks
  • Belt drive system design and Ackermann steering geometry optimization
  • 3D printing with PLA and hands-on assembly
  • Project management and scheduling
  • Performance testing and iterative refinement

Results

The RC car successfully completed 3 laps of the designated circuit within the required 20-second time limit, earning a perfect score on the project. Its PLA chassis and belt drive system provided consistent power delivery, while the servo-controlled Ackermann steering ensured precise handling through turns. The project was delivered on time with full functionality.

Windmill Generator

Team Lead

Objective

Lead a team in developing a functional wind-powered generator system with integrated electrical monitoring as part of a Mechatronics lab final project. The goal was to convert wind energy into usable DC voltage and visually indicate fan performance through an analog-electronic interface.

Skills Used

  • Analog circuit design with diodes, transistors, and comparators
  • Signal smoothing and AC to DC voltage conversion
  • Voltage threshold detection using resistor dividers and potentiometers
  • Team collaboration and troubleshooting in lab environments
  • LED-based feedback system implementation

Results

The windmill circuit successfully converted AC fan input into DC voltage and provided intuitive visual feedback via a tri-color LED system. Threshold detection using comparators allowed for real-time indication of low, normal, and high fan speeds. The project overcame early design challenges and was completed with reliable functionality and improved design efficiency using IC components.

"MIKU" Battle Bot

Ground-Game Engineer

Objective

Design and build a fully functional combat robot featuring a high-speed spinning saw blade and servo-actuated hammer arm, optimized for precision strikes in competitive matches. The bot had to be cost-effective, highly maneuverable, and capable of pinning down and disabling opponents using custom attachments.

Skills Used

  • Onshape for complex servo-driven mechanical design
  • 3D printing with TPU for flexible, impact-resistant parts
  • Rapid prototyping and iterative assembly under budget constraints
  • Custom fork and weapon integration for competitive match control
  • Combat performance testing and design for field reparability

Results

The final robot, Miku, was 95% 3D printed in TPU and stayed precisely within the $300 budget. It featured a 300 mph saw blade mounted on an actuated servo arm and demonstrated precise cutting capabilities during matches. Miku's custom forks effectively pinned down opponents, allowing for controlled engagements. The project was praised for its strategic design, maneuvarability, and match-ready reliability.
